Apprenticeships

 

Who will pay the levy

From 1 May 2017, if you are an employer with a payroll of more than £3 million, the way you access funding for apprenticeships will change.

Find out about the apprenticeship levy in more detail.

If you're not a levy payer

If you’re not a levy payer you will continue to share the cost of apprenticeships with the government. Find out more about employing an apprentice.

How much levy will you pay

  • is based on your total payroll bill over the year, which excludes agency workers
  • will be charged at a rate of 0.5% of your annual payroll bill
  • will be reduced by an allowance of £15,000 per year
  • you will pay the levy to HM Revenue and Customs (HMRC) through the PAYE process

The government will apply a 10% monthly top-up to the funds you have for spending on apprenticeships training in England.

Example of funding

  • an employer with an annual payroll bill of £5,000,000
  • levy sum: 0.5% x £5,000,000 = £25,000
  • subtracting levy allowance: £25,000 - £15,000 = £10,000 annual levy payment
