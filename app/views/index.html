Apprenticeships

From 1 May 2017, if you are an employer with a payroll of more than £3 million, the way you access funding for apprenticeships will change.

Find out about the apprenticeship levy in more detail.

You will be able to access your apprenticeship funding through the digital apprenticeship service where you can:

  1. 1Estimate your apprenticeship funding to help calculate how much you’ll have to spend on apprenticeship training and the cost.
  2. 2Choose an apprenticeship training for your business and then find a training provider.
  3. 3Work with your training provider to advertise an apprenticeship.

If you pay the levy you can

  1. 4Register securely using your Government Gateway ID and companies house number (which payroll or finance should have).
  2. 5Create and manage your levy account.
  3. 6Link PAYE schemes to create your account.
  4. 7Agree a cost for training with your training provider and start a new apprenticeship contract.
  5. 8Authorise the payments to your training provider and manage your account.

If you’re not a levy payer you will continue to share the cost of apprenticeships with the government. Find out more about employing an apprentice.

The levy

  • is based on your total payroll bill over the year, which excludes agency workers
  • will be charged at a rate of 0.5% of your annual payroll bill
  • will be reduced by an allowance of £15,000 per year
  • will be paid to HM Revenue and Customs (HMRC) through the PAYE process

The government will apply a 10% monthly top-up to the funds you have for spending on apprenticeships training in England.

Example of what levy you will pay

An employer with an annual payroll bill of £5,000,000:

  • levy sum: 0.5% x £5,000,000 = £25,000
  • subtracting levy allowance: £25,000 - £15,000 = £10,000 annual levy payment
