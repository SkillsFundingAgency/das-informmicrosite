Use this guidance to set up and use your apprenticeship levy account.

Contents

 

How the levy works

To pay training providers through your levy funds you must have an annual payroll bill that exceeds £3 million for any given financial year.

HM Revenue and Customs (HMRC) will collect your levy payments through your PAYE at a rate of 0.5% per year.

The levy will be reduced by an allowance of £15,000 per year. The government will also apply a 10% monthly top-up to the funds you have for spending on apprenticeships training in England.

Example of what levy you will pay:

An employer with an annual payroll bill of £5,000,000:

  • levy sum: 0.5% x £5,000,000 = £25,000
  • subtracting levy allowance: £25,000 - £15,000 = £10,000 annual levy payment

If your payroll changes due to seasonal staff changes or bonuses, it could affect your levy contributions and HMRC declarations for that period.

Understand the digital apprenticeship service

The digital apprenticeship service gives you greater control of your apprenticeships and management of your levy funds. You’ll be able to use this service to:

  • pay for the training and assessment of apprentices in England
  • find providers to help deliver your apprenticeships

Who should use it

You should use this guidance to help you set up and manage your account on the digital apprenticeship service.

Ideally someone from finance, payroll, legal or HR.

All account users should understand the guidance before using the digital apprenticeship service as it will help you to operate within the terms set out in the Apprenticeship Service Conditions of Use [add link].

Before you start

To access your funds you’ll need:

  • someone to set up the account (the account holder)
  • login details of your Government Gateway ID
  • a PAYE scheme

The service gives you flexibility to manage your PAYE schemes under one account or set up multiple accounts, 1 for each scheme.

Set up your account

You can assign different people to do different tasks within your account.

Account owner

If you set up the account, you’re the account owner.

The account owner can:

  • start and stop apprenticeships
  • start and stop payments
  • sign new contracts with training providers
  • view all financial information, such as current balances and forecasts
  • add or remove PAYE schemes from the account
  • manage who has access to the account and what they can do
  • sign an SFA agreement on behalf of each organisation that has an account

The account owner needs to sign the SFA agreement before the account can be used to hire apprentices.

The SFA agreement is a legal agreement that the account owner must sign on behalf of the organisation using the account.

The SFA agreement must be signed in order for you to spend levy funds.

Manage apprenticeships

As the account owner, you can invite people to manage apprenticeships and payments within the account.

These people can:

  • start and stop apprenticeships
  • start and stop payments
  • sign new contracts with training providers
  • view all financial information, such as current balances and forecasts

View only

As the account owner, you can invite people to view all financial aspects of the account. They won’t be able to change any information on the account.

Add a PAYE scheme

You need to add a PAYE to your account to claim funding from that PAYE scheme. You can set up your account using one or multiple PAYE schemes.

  • your levy payments are worked out from your payroll and are linked to individual PAYE schemes
  • you can’t use a PAYE scheme on more than 1 account
  • when you add a PAYE scheme to your account your levy funds will automatically show as funds available
  • to add a PAYE scheme you need to enter your Government Gateway credentials and sign a user declaration.

Examples of different PAYE schemes

scenario 1:

1 account, 1 organisation and 1 PAYE scheme

  • an organisation has no other connected organisation
  • as long as the employer agreement has been signed, the organisation will be able to pay for apprenticeship training from the account

scenario 2:

1 account, 2 organisations and 1 PAYE scheme

  • organisation A and organisation B have been linked to 1 account with 1 PAYE scheme
  • both organisations will have to sign employer agreements if they want to make apprenticeship payments from the account

scenario 3:

1 account, 2 organisations and 2 PAYE schemes

  • organisation A and organisation B have been linked to 1 account with 2 PAYE schemes
  • The levy payments from 2 PAYE schemes are shown in the one account but they are not divided between the 2 organisations
  • both organisations will have to sign employer agreements if they want to make apprenticeship payments from the account

scenario 4:

2 accounts with 2 organisations and 2 PAYE schemes linked to each account

  • 2 digital accounts have been created. In the accounts, each has 2 legal entities and 2 PAYE schemes where all employer agreements have been signed
  • in 1 account the 2 organisations have pooled their funds from the 2 PAYE schemes to make apprenticeship payments from that account
  • in the second account the organisations have done the same thing as the first account

scenario 5:

2 accounts, 1 organisation with 1 or more PAYE schemes

  • 2 accounts have been created for 1 organisation, but different PAYE schemes are linked to each account
  • You can choose to have a organisation linked to more than 1 account if you have multiple divisions or cost centres - this lets you manage your apprenticeship programmes separately
  • You can’t split a PAYE credit across 2 accounts
  • The apprenticeship agreement must be made via the account that has the linked PAYE scheme through which the apprentice is to be paid

Connected organisations

You can manage connected companies’ levy funds in the most suitable way to meet your needs and company structure.

For example, in 1 account you could:

  • have multiple connected organisations
  • pool funds from linked PAYE schemes – see paragraph 35 of HMRC guidance (LINK)

Add an apprentice

Once your account is set up, you can release funds.

To do this:

  • add an apprentice
  • review and approve apprentices

You can now add apprentices to your account

But first you need to know:

  • who your training provider will be and have an agreement in place to deliver the training
  • the company that your apprentices will be employed by, if you have multiple companies

When you're in your account you, or your training provider need to:

  • enter the cost of the apprenticeship training, as agreed with both you and your training provider
  • agree the duration of the apprenticeship, which can be changed during the apprenticeship

Review and approve apprentices

Check:

  • apprentices' details
  • funds

Understand your account funds

Once your account is set up you will see:

  • your account will have a year to date (YTD) levy value each month - this will be shown in your balance
  • the value will usually increase each month, but it could also go down
  • the payment for your apprenticeship training will be taken from the latest balance
  • if the balance is fully spent you will need to make the 10% co-investment payment as outlined in the funding rules [link] - this will happen automatically
  • you can make changes to your HMRC returns for up to 6 years
  • any changes to previous months will be recorded in your account as a reconciliation for that period

Examples:

Example 1:

In month 12 the declaration for the YTD levy was £120,000. In July it was changed to £135,000 adding an additional £15,000. This additional funding was included in the July credit and levy balance.

Additional declaration £15,000

July credit £10,000

Total credit £25,000

A 10% top up of £2,500 was also applied to the balance.

Example 2:

In month 12 the declaration for the YTD levy of £120,000. In July it was changed to £95,000 deducting £25,000 from the end of year declaration. This deduction is included with the July credit and levy balance.

Negative declaration - £25,000

July credit £10,000

Total credit £15,000

The 10% top up of - £1,500 would also show as a negative figure as illustrated below.

Negative declaration (10%) - £2,500

July credit (10%) £1,000

Total credit - £1,500
